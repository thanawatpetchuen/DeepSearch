                <h1>eBooks  and Texts</h1>
                  <h4>Internet Archive</h4>
                <div id="descript" style="max-height:43px;cursor:pointer;"
         onclick="event && event.target && event.target.nodeName.toLowerCase()!='a' && $('#tabby-about-finder').click()"
        >
          <p><img src="/download/texts/texts_archive.jpg" max-width="25%" style="float:right; padding: 0 5px 5px 5px;">  

The Internet Archive offers over <b>15,000,000</b>&nbsp;freely downloadable books and texts. There is also a collection of <a href="https://archive.org/details/inlibrary">550,000 modern eBooks</a> that may be borrowed by anyone with a free archive.org account.<br>
<br>
<a style="padding:5px;display:block;width:200px;float:center;text-align:center;margin:10px;background:#666633 ;color:#FFFFFF;font-weight:bold;border-radius:5px;" href="https://archive.org/details/inlibrary">Borrow a Book</a>
<br>Books on Internet Archive are offered in many formats, including DAISY files intended for print disabled people. &nbsp;In addition to the collections here, print disabled people may access <a href="https://openlibrary.org/subjects/protected_daisy#ebooks=true&amp;sort=edition_count">a large collection of modern books</a> provided as encrypted DAISY files on OpenLibrary.org. Please <a href="https://openlibrary.org/help/faq/accessing">visit the FAQ</a> for more information about DAISY files and how to un-encrypt them.&nbsp;<br>
<br>
</p><p>
<a style="padding:5px;display:block;width:200px;float:center;text-align:center;margin:10px;background:#666633 ;color:#FFFFFF;font-weight:bold;border-radius:5px;" href="http://archive.org/scanning">Get Something Digitized</a>
<br>
The Internet Archive also encourages libraries, content holders and the reading community at large, to have their printed materials non-destructively digitized and put online for the benefit of all. The Internet Archive has digitized over  <b>2.1 million books</b> and microforms. Both large and small collections of monographs, serials, archival materials, maps, diaries and photographs to name a few,  can be digitized in over <b>33 global scanning centers</b> found on 4 continents. For more information please visit <a href="http://archive.org/scanning">http://archive.org/scanning</a>.<br><br>
Alternatively, our portable Table Top Scanner can also be purchased and used on-site within libraries and archives. To read more about our TT Scribe, please visit <a href="http://archive.org/details/tabletopscribesystem">www.archive.org/details/tabletopscribesystem</a>.<br>
<br>
Since 2005, the Internet Archive has collaborated and built digital collections with over <b>1,100 Library Institutions</b> and other content providers. Partnerships include: <a href="https://archive.org/details/bostonpubliclibrary">Boston Public Library</a>, the <a href="https://archive.org/details/library_of_congress">Library of Congress</a> and the <a href="https://archive.org/details/lancaster_county">Lancaster County's Historical Society</a>.  These collections are digitized from various mediatypes including: <a href="http://archive.org/details/microfilm">microfilm and microfiche</a>, <a href="http://archive.org/details/wellesleynews">journals and serial publications</a>, and a wide variety of <a href="http://archive.org/details/archivalcollections">archival material</a>. Significant contributions have come from partners in North America (<a href="https://archive.org/details/americana">American</a> and <a href="https://archive.org/details/toronto">Canadian</a> Libraries), <a href="https://archive.org/details/europeanlibraries">Europe</a> and <a href="https://archive.org/details/universallibrary">Asia</a>, representing more than 184 languages.<br>
<br>

<a style="padding:5px;display:block;width:200px;float:center;text-align:center;margin:10px;background:#666633 ;color:#FFFFFF;font-weight:bold;border-radius:5px;" href="https://archive.org/upload/">Contribute a Book</a>
<br>

 The Internet Archive encourages our global community to contribute physical items, as well as uploading digital materials directly to the Internet Archive. If you have digital items that you would like to add to the Internet Archive, please&nbsp;<a href="https://archive.org/create/">Create</a> a new item using the uploader interface. Click here to apply the specific creative commons license <a href="http://creativecommons.org/choose/">Creative Commons</a> license to communicate how the material can be used. <br>
<br>
<b>For donation of physical books or items, please contact</b> <ahref="mailto:info@archive.org">info@archive.org <br>

          <p><img src="/download/texts/texts_archive.jpg" max-width="25%" style="float:right; padding: 0 5px 5px 5px;">  

The Internet Archive offers over <b>15,000,000</b>&nbsp;freely downloadable books and texts. There is also a collection of <a href="https://archive.org/details/inlibrary">550,000 modern eBooks</a> that may be borrowed by anyone with a free archive.org account.<br>
<br>
<a style="padding:5px;display:block;width:200px;float:center;text-align:center;margin:10px;background:#666633 ;color:#FFFFFF;font-weight:bold;border-radius:5px;" href="https://archive.org/details/inlibrary">Borrow a Book</a>
<br>Books on Internet Archive are offered in many formats, including DAISY files intended for print disabled people. &nbsp;In addition to the collections here, print disabled people may access <a href="https://openlibrary.org/subjects/protected_daisy#ebooks=true&amp;sort=edition_count">a large collection of modern books</a> provided as encrypted DAISY files on OpenLibrary.org. Please <a href="https://openlibrary.org/help/faq/accessing">visit the FAQ</a> for more information about DAISY files and how to un-encrypt them.&nbsp;<br>
<br>
</p><p>
<a style="padding:5px;display:block;width:200px;float:center;text-align:center;margin:10px;background:#666633 ;color:#FFFFFF;font-weight:bold;border-radius:5px;" href="http://archive.org/scanning">Get Something Digitized</a>
<br>
The Internet Archive also encourages libraries, content holders and the reading community at large, to have their printed materials non-destructively digitized and put online for the benefit of all. The Internet Archive has digitized over  <b>2.1 million books</b> and microforms. Both large and small collections of monographs, serials, archival materials, maps, diaries and photographs to name a few,  can be digitized in over <b>33 global scanning centers</b> found on 4 continents. For more information please visit <a href="http://archive.org/scanning">http://archive.org/scanning</a>.<br><br>
Alternatively, our portable Table Top Scanner can also be purchased and used on-site within libraries and archives. To read more about our TT Scribe, please visit <a href="http://archive.org/details/tabletopscribesystem">www.archive.org/details/tabletopscribesystem</a>.<br>
<br>
Since 2005, the Internet Archive has collaborated and built digital collections with over <b>1,100 Library Institutions</b> and other content providers. Partnerships include: <a href="https://archive.org/details/bostonpubliclibrary">Boston Public Library</a>, the <a href="https://archive.org/details/library_of_congress">Library of Congress</a> and the <a href="https://archive.org/details/lancaster_county">Lancaster County's Historical Society</a>.  These collections are digitized from various mediatypes including: <a href="http://archive.org/details/microfilm">microfilm and microfiche</a>, <a href="http://archive.org/details/wellesleynews">journals and serial publications</a>, and a wide variety of <a href="http://archive.org/details/archivalcollections">archival material</a>. Significant contributions have come from partners in North America (<a href="https://archive.org/details/americana">American</a> and <a href="https://archive.org/details/toronto">Canadian</a> Libraries), <a href="https://archive.org/details/europeanlibraries">Europe</a> and <a href="https://archive.org/details/universallibrary">Asia</a>, representing more than 184 languages.<br>

<a style="padding:5px;display:block;width:200px;float:center;text-align:center;margin:10px;background:#666633 ;color:#FFFFFF;font-weight:bold;border-radius:5px;" href="https://archive.org/upload/">Contribute a Book</a>
<br>

 The Internet Archive encourages our global community to contribute physical items, as well as uploading digital materials directly to the Internet Archive.
