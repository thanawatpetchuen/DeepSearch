<h1>Author and Citation Information for "The Modern History of Computing"</h1>    <p>
	The latest version of the entry "<a href="https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/computing-history" class="plain">The Modern History of Computing</a>"
	may be cited via the earliest archive in which this version appears:
	</p>
	<blockquote>
	Copeland, B. Jack, "The Modern History of Computing", <em>The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy </em> (Winter 2017 Edition), Edward N. Zalta&nbsp;(ed.),
	 URL = &lt;https://plato.stanford.edu/archives/win2017/entries/computing-history/&gt;.
	</blockquote>

	The citation above refers to the version in the following archive edition:
	<ul>
	<li><a href="https://plato.stanford.edu/archives/win2017/entries/computing-history/" class="plain">Winter 2017</a> (minor correction)</li>
	</ul>
<p>
Please note that you are being asked to cite a URL that is in a fixed,
archived edition of the encyclopedia.
The reason for this is that the  Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy 
recommends that readers cite
a stable document for scholarly purposes. You should not cite the
dynamic portion of the encyclopedia.</p>
